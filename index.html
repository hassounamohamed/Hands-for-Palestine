History of Palestine

In 1967, the Israeli army occupied the West Bank of the Jordan River, which was at that time part of Jordan. They also occupied the Gaza Strip, the Sinai Peninsula, the Syrian Golan Heights, and other areas in the north of Jordan. This war became known as the Six-Day War, and it entered the Palestinian lexicon as the 'Naksa' (Setback). Israel occupied Sinai.
